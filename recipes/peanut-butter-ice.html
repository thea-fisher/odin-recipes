Peanut Butter Ice Cream Lollies

The Perfect Post-Workout Treat! This cooling post-workout treat is the perfect way to finish a hot summer session. It takes just minutes to make. Simply throw everything into a blender, pour into moulds, and pop in the freezer. You will be enjoying one of these in no time.

Perfect for meal prep, made in 5 steps, under 300kcal per lolly and packed with 12g of protein per serving! Just scale up the ingridents to your desired quantity and serve with fresh fruit, a protein shake or smoothie bowl.

All you will need is ice lolly moulds, lollypop sticks, a handheld blender and the ingredients listed below.

ingredients:

  • 1.5 ripe bananas
  • 100g yoghurt
  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder of your choice
  • 30g of peanut butter of your choice - we reccomand an all natural brand
  • 15g maple syrup

Steps:

  1. Add the banana, yoghurt, protein powder, peanut butter and maple syrup to a large bowl.
  2. With a handheld blender, combine the ingredients until you have a smooth, thick mixture.
  3. Grab your moulds and fill with chopped peanuts, dark chocolate and salt.
  4. Put the ice cream sticks into place and pour the ice cream mixture into the moulds, over your toppings.
  5. Freeze until solid
  6. Enjoy post-workout!

High Protein Chicken and Chickpea Salad

A fresh and colourful, high protein chicken salad for two, perfect as a relaxed summer lunch or packed into a picnic. By chopping all the ingredients into bite-size pieces, every mouthful becomes a different mix of flavours and textures.

For a healthier version of this chicken salad, you can swap the mayonnaise for plain yoghurt. You can cook the chicken from fresh but it also works brilliantly with leftovers from a roast. Each serving provides 633 kcal, 68.3g protein, 25.3g carbohydrate (of which 6.1g sugars), 26.4g fat (of which 2.9g saturates), 10.9g fibre and 0.32g salt.

ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts - boneless and skinless
  • 1tbsp rapeseed or olive oil
  • 400g tinned chickpeas
  • 2 spring onions - finely sliced
  • 10 cherry tomatoes - cut into quarters
  • Quarter cucumber - cut into cubes
  • 10g fresh mint leaves - finely chopped
  • 10g fresh parsley leaves - finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 2 small handfulls of mixed salad leaves
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Season the chicken breasts both sides with salt and pepper. Place a frying pan on a medium heat and add the oil. Cook the breasts for 6 to 8 minutes on each side or until cooked through then transfer them to a plate to rest for a few minutes and cool slightly.
  2. In a sieve, drain and lightly rinse the chickpeas then give a little shake to dry. Tip them into a large mixing bowl and stir in the lemon zest and juice.
  3. Add in the spring onions, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, mint, parsley and mayonnaise, season with salt and pepper then mix well.
  4. Slice the chicken into bite-sized pieces and add to the bowl along with the salad leaves. Mix again then serve.
